Cat Friendly Vet

We are very proud to announce that Vet has achieved Silver accreditation as a Cat Friendly Clinic (CFC). The CFC programme has been developed by the International Society of Feline Medicine, the veterinary division of the leading feline charity International Cat Care. The CFC programme aims to promote well-being and high standards of care for all cats visiting or being hospitalised in a veterinary clinic. A clinic has to prove rigorous adherence to a set of criteria which includes provision of facilities and demonstration of staff activities and attitudes aimed at reducing stress in cats, both as in-patients and out-patients.

Our Team

Joanna Jones

Joanna is a Board Certified Specialist in Veterinary Anaesthesia and Analgesia. Specialist veterinary anaesthetists have advanced training in all aspects of anaesthesiaand pain management in a wide range of species. Joanna graduated from Sydney University in 2008 and worked in mixed and emergency practice in Australia and overseas. After completing an equine internship she stayed in equine referral practice where she developed her love of anaesthesia.

Perrine Stone

Perrine qualified from the University of Liverpool in 2007 and went on to spend three years in mixed practice in North Wales before gaining a further two years’ experience in emergency and critical care and as a locum. This was followed by five years in charity practice. She completed her residency in veterinary anaesthesia and analgesia in 2020, then gained further experience in teaching and anaesthesia practice as a Lecturer in Veterinary Anaesthesia at the University of Liverpool. Perrine joined us here at Vet in 2022, and provides an individualised, safe, stress-free and comfortable hospital experience for her patients.
